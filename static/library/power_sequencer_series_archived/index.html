    Blender Power Sequencer tutorials

    Learn to edit videos with Blender's Video Sequence Editor. And edit much faster with our Free and open source add-on Power Sequencer! With this playlist, you'll learn fundamental Blender video editing techniques on top of Power Sequencer's unique features.

    Learn Video Editing in Blender: Getting started with the VSE and Power Sequencer

    In this tutorial, Daniel and I run you through the basics of video editing with both the vanilla Blender and our open source add-on Power Sequencer!

    Power Sequencer Workflow 1

    Learn to edit videos faster in the Blender VSE with Power Sequencer.

    Import local footage

    The add-on imports video, audio and image files instantly as long as they're next to your .blend file.

    Cut and Trim with the Mouse

    Learn to cut and trim video strips fast with mouse clicks!

    Crossfades

    Crossfades are soft transitions from one clip to the next. With Power Sequencer, press Ctrl Alt C to auto crossfade to the next strip.

    Fade in, fade out

    The Blender add-on adds easy to control fades, or opacity transitions. F to fade in and out, Ctrl F to fade in only, and Alt F to fade out only.

    Render for the web

    Auto name and export your videos for Youtube, Facebook, Twitter in one key press from the Blender Sequencer. More render presets to come!

    Install Python and FFmpeg on Windows

    Learn to install Python, but also FFmpeg to process videos, what the PATH is, and how to make FFmpeg globally accessible using the PATH environment variable.
